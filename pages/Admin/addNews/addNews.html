Prasidh Krishna tests positive; England tour in jeopardy

Prasidh Krishna is the latest from the Indian Premier League (IPL) to test positive for COVID-19. The news comes less than 24 hours after he was selected for India's tour of England as a standby player.

Cricbuzz has learnt that the Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) pacer was negative while he was with the franchise in Ahmedabad where a couple of their players - Varun Chakravarthy and Sandeep Warrier - had returned positive tests. However, sources in the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) and KKR revealed to this website that Krishna tested positive on returning home in Bangalore.

India leave out Hardik, Shaw for WTC Final, England Tests

India have left out Hardik Pandya, Prithvi Shaw and Kuldeep Yadav from their squad for their World Test Championship final against New Zealand as well as the five-Test tour of England that follows. Hardik, whose workload has been constantly managed since a back injury in 2018, nursed a shoulder niggle during the IPL 2021 that kept him off any bowling duties in the seven games for Mumbai Indians. The all-rounder last played a Test on the previous tour of England in 2018.

Squad: Rohit Sharma, Shubman Gill, Mayank Agarwal, Cheteshwar Pujara, Virat Kohli (Captain), Ajinkya Rahane (vice-captain), Hanuma Vihari, Rishabh Pant (wicket-keeper), R Ashwin, Ravindra Jadeja, Axar Patel.

Bio-Secure Environments are never foolproof: Smith empathises with BCCI over IPL situation

Maybe because he and Sourav Ganguly go back, perhaps because he knows what it feels like to have the plug pulled on an ongoing event, possibly because it's never a bad idea to say good things about the BCCI, but Graeme Smith had empathy and approval for how India's cricket bosses are dealing with the grim-reaping crisis that has engulfed the IPL.

"In no way would we judge," Smith, CSA's director of cricket, told an online press conference on Thursday. "Having spoken to the players, they felt safe. They felt the BSE [Bio-Secure Environment] was a really good experience in India. They never felt at risk. But that's the nature of what Covid brings."

Williamson & Co. fly out of Delhi mini-bubble to Maldives

The New Zealand cricketers at IPL 2021 seem to be creating curiosity among not just Indian managers of the game but back home in New Zealand Cricket (NZC) too. A day after four members of the New Zealand national team, including skipper Kane Williamson, were placed in a mini bio-secure bubble in New Delhi, it has emerged that the four New Zealanders are actually are in Maldives and not in India. What's more, NZC does not seem to be aware of their exact whereabouts.

As previously reported by Cricbuzz, Williamson, Kyle Jamieson, Mitchell Santner and physio Tommy Simsek were made part of a secure bio-safety net in Delhi with full knowledge of NZC on Thursday.
